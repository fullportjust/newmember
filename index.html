Account setup — common questions3 questions+

Do I need to apply for options?

Yes. Apply when you open your brokerage — you can't trade options until you're approved. Do it early so you're not waiting.

I can't find today's (same-day) expiration on Robinhood

Turn it on: Settings → Investing → Options → enable "Trade on expiration date." Most other brokers already have it on.

Can I day-trade with under $25,000?

Yes — switch to a cash account to skip the day-trading limit (the "PDT rule"). You trade with settled cash — what you have at the open. Sell a position to free that cash; it settles back overnight.

Part 2 · Copy our trades

Every trade we post uses the same 4 tags. Learn them and you can copy any call.

[BUY]

Our entry. We post the stock, strike, expiration date, and the price we got in at. Get in as close to our price as you can.

[TRIM]

Take some profit — usually half. Try to Trim around the same time we do. Only one contract? This is your Sell.

[SELL]

Take the rest of the profit. The winner's closed — we're out.

[CLOSE]

Cut a loss. Losses happen — it's normal. We'll tell you when to take one. Close it, or hold at your own risk.

Entry rule: get in as close to our [BUY] price as you can. If it's already 10–15% above our entry, skip it and wait for the next call. Don't chase.

Golden rule: Trim into profit, then move your stop to breakeven — so a winner can't turn into a loss.

Trades & signals — common questions5 questions+

How do I follow a signal?

First, know the parts of a contract (below) and how to find them on your broker. A signal looks like [BUY] TSLA 3/8 $180 Call @ 2.00. Then follow the tags: [BUY] buy it · [TRIM] sell part to bank profit · [SELL] sell the rest · [CLOSE] cut a loss.

TRIM vs SELL — what's the difference?

TRIM = sell some (usually half) to lock in profit while keeping a little upside. SELL = sell it all, you're fully out. Example: hold 5 contracts → a TRIM might sell 1–2; a SELL closes all 5. Only have 1 contract? Treat the first TRIM as your sell.

Stocks vs options — what's the difference?

A stock is owning part of a company. An option is a contract tied to that stock's price — you trade the contract, not the shares. One Call contract is the right to buy 100 shares at a set price, but you never actually have to. You buy contracts cheap and sell them higher.

What's a Call and a Put?

A Call bets the stock goes up. A Put bets it goes down. Example: TSLA 3/8 $180 Call bets Tesla is above $180 by March 8.

What do the parts of a contract mean?

Format: [Stock] [Expiration] $[Strike] [Call/Put] @ [Price]. Example: TSLA 3/8 $180 Call @ 2.00 — stock TSLA, expires 3/8, strike $180, a Call, price 2.00. Each contract = 100 shares, so 2.00 = $200.

Phase 03 — Non-negotiable

Risk & portfolio management.

The most important part. Good entries don't keep you alive — sizing does. Most people don't blow up because they're wrong. They blow up because they're too big when they're wrong.

Video · Sizing, risk & protecting your portfolio
Size every trade as a % of your account — and assume it can go to $0. Options die worthless all the time. If a full loss would hurt, you're too big.

Position sizing

  • Full — 3–5% of your account (default)
  • Small — 1–3% when you're less sure
  • Lotto — under 1%, high risk, can go to $0
  • Small account? Scale up a bit — but don't chase junk contracts

Risk by time left

  • 1–7 days — most dangerous, loses value fast
  • 8–14 days — still risky
  • 15–30 days — more forgiving
  • 30+ days — lowest risk, more time to be right
Protect your wins. Trim into profit. Move your stop to breakeven. Never let a green trade go red. Survive first — the gains add up when you're still here next month.
Risk & sizing — common questions3 questions+

How much per trade? What's a "lotto"?

Size by % of your account: Full 3–5%, Small 1–3%, Lotto under 1% (high risk, can go to $0). Small account? You may need a higher % — don't chase $0.05 contracts just to stay "lotto" size; they rarely hit.

What's your take-profit / stop-loss plan?

Manual trailing take-profit: enter with a few contracts, [TRIM] about half at ~20–40% profit, then move your stop to breakeven so it can't turn into a loss. Hard stop around -50%, but we usually [SELL] well before that.

How does time-to-expiration change the risk?

Less time = more risk. 1–7 days ultra-high (loses value fast) · 8–14 high · 15–30 moderate · 31–60 low-moderate · 60+ lowest (LEAPS, long-term).

Staying safe from scams — common questions2 questions+

Will an admin ever DM me?

No. We never DM you first, and we never run a "copy trader" or investment fund. Any DM like that is a scam. Tip: turn off friend requests. To verify someone, click our name inside the Discord and message from there. Check usernames exactly — it's "traderkenny," not "_traderkenny" or "traderkennyy."

Someone's DMing me pretending to be Kenny — is it real?

Almost never. Scammers copy the name and photo, act friendly, then send you to a fake site (never Whop.com). Only trust a chat you start yourself by clicking the real name inside our Discord.
